Project Shadow Mapping

3D Graphics · WebGL

Project Overview

Shadow mapping is a two-pass technique: in the first pass the scene is rendered from the light's point of view into a depth texture (shadow map); in the second pass each fragment checks its light-space depth against the stored value to determine if it is in shadow. This project implements shadow mapping from a directional light with a configurable position.

Tasks Completed

  • Created a dedicated framebuffer with a depth texture attachment for the shadow pass.
  • Rendered the scene geometry into the depth buffer from an orthographic light-space projection.
  • In the lighting pass, transformed each fragment into light space and sampled the shadow map.
  • Applied a percentage-closer filtering (PCF) kernel to soften shadow edges.
  • Added a configurable bias to eliminate shadow acne caused by self-shadowing.

Reflection

Shadow acne and peter-panning are two sides of the same coin — increase bias to remove acne and the shadow detaches from its caster. Finding the right balance required understanding the relationship between polygon slope, light angle, and shadow-map texel size. PCF dramatically improves visual quality at minimal extra cost.
